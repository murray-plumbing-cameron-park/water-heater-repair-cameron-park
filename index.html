Key Terminology

Thermostatic control
The mechanism that regulates target water temperature by cycling heating on and off, usually through a thermostat and related sensors or controls.
Temperature and pressure relief valve
A safety device intended to open under unsafe conditions to reduce risk from excessive temperature or pressure within a storage tank.
Sediment accumulation
Mineral deposits that settle in a tank or heat exchanger and can reduce efficiency, alter temperature stability, and increase component stress.
Ignition or heating element
The primary heat source: a burner/ignition assembly in gas systems or an electrical element in electric systems, both requiring verification during diagnostics.
Dielectric connection
A fitting or union designed to reduce galvanic corrosion when dissimilar metals meet within the water distribution system.
